Types of Driveway Cracks & Solutions

LOW

Hairline Cracks

Less than 1/8 inch wide

Characteristics:
  • Surface-level cracks in sealcoat
  • Usually straight or slightly curved
  • Don't extend through full asphalt depth
  • Common in newly sealed driveways
Repair Method:
  • Crack sealing: Liquid crack filler
  • Surface preparation: Clean and dry cracks
  • Application: Brush-in or pour sealant
  • Finishing: Blend with surface
Cost: $200-400 ‚Ä¢ Longevity: 3-5 years
MEDIUM

Structural Cracks

1/4 to 1/2 inch wide

Characteristics:
  • Extend through full asphalt thickness
  • Often follow straight lines
  • May show slight vertical displacement
  • Allow water penetration to base
Repair Method:
  • Hot-applied crack filler: Rubberized material
  • Crack routing: Create reservoir for filler
  • Professional application: Heated material
  • Surface finishing: Squeegee level
Cost: $400-600 ‚Ä¢ Longevity: 4-6 years
HIGH

Working Cracks

Active movement cracks

Characteristics:
  • Open and close with temperature changes
  • Often caused by clay soil movement
  • May show seasonal variation
  • Previous repairs may have failed
Repair Method:
  • Flexible sealants: Accommodate movement
  • Backer rod installation: Control fill depth
  • Premium materials: High-movement capability
  • Professional assessment: Address underlying causes
Cost: $500-700 ‚Ä¢ Longevity: 3-5 years
CRITICAL

Severe Cracks

Over 1/2 inch or multiple cracks

Characteristics:
  • Wide gaps or multiple interconnected cracks
  • Significant vertical displacement
  • Base material may be visible
  • Potential safety hazard
Repair Method:
  • Comprehensive repair: May require patching
  • Base stabilization: Address underlying issues
  • Multi-step process: Prepare, fill, seal
  • Professional evaluation: Consider replacement options
