Seal Coating Basics

What is seal coating and why is it important?

Seal coating is a protective liquid coating applied to asphalt surfaces that acts as a barrier against:

  • Water penetration - Prevents freeze-thaw damage and base erosion
  • UV rays - Stops sun damage that causes cracking and fading
  • Chemicals - Protects against oil, gas, and salt damage
  • Weather elements - Guards against rain, snow, and temperature extremes
Key Benefits:
  • Extends pavement life by 200-300%
  • Reduces repair costs by up to 80%
  • Improves curb appeal and property value
  • Provides slip-resistant surface texture

What types of seal coating are available?

Coal Tar Emulsion

Best for: High-traffic commercial areas, gas stations

Pros: Superior chemical resistance, long-lasting

Cons: Environmental concerns, limited availability

Asphalt Emulsion

Best for: Residential driveways, parking lots

Pros: Eco-friendly, good adhesion, cost-effective

Cons: Less chemical resistance than coal tar

Acrylic Sealers

Best for: Decorative surfaces, tennis courts

Pros: UV resistance, color options, fast drying

Cons: Higher cost, requires frequent reapplication

Can I seal coat over existing seal coating?

Yes, but timing and condition matter:

  • Wait 6-12 months after previous application for full cure
  • Surface must be clean - remove oil stains, debris, vegetation
  • Check for peeling - remove loose or failing coating first
  • Maximum 3-4 coats - too many layers can crack and peel
Warning: Never apply new seal coating over wet, dirty, or severely deteriorated surfaces. Professional assessment recommended for surfaces with multiple previous coats.

Timing & Scheduling

How often should I seal coat my driveway?

Recommended Frequency by Usage:

Residential Driveways
  • Light traffic (1-2 cars daily): Every 3-4 years
  • Moderate traffic (3-4 cars daily): Every 2-3 years
  • Heavy traffic (5+ cars daily): Every 2 years
Commercial Properties
  • Office parking (daytime only): Every 2-3 years
  • Retail centers (constant traffic): Every 1-2 years
  • Industrial sites (heavy vehicles): Annually

Factors That Affect Frequency:

  • Texas sun exposure - South-facing surfaces need more frequent treatment
  • Tree coverage - Shade extends seal coating life
  • Vehicle types - Heavy trucks accelerate wear
  • Chemical exposure - Oil leaks require more frequent sealing
  • Drainage quality - Standing water reduces coating life

What's the best time of year for seal coating in Texas?

Spring (March - May) - OPTIMAL

  • Ideal temperatures: 60-80°F
  • Lower humidity for better curing
  • Minimal rain in late spring
  • Advantage: Full summer protection

Fall (September - November) - GOOD

  • Cooler temperatures: 65-85°F
  • Less UV stress during curing
  • Advantage: Winter damage prevention
  • Drawback: Shorter curing season

Summer (June - August) - AVOID

  • Excessive heat: 90-105°F
  • Rapid drying prevents proper adhesion
  • UV damage during application
  • Worker safety concerns

Winter (December - February) - AVOID

  • Cold temperatures: Below 50°F
  • Poor adhesion and curing
  • Moisture issues from rain/frost
  • Extended drying times

How long does seal coating take to dry and cure?

Typical Drying Timeline:

Initial Set (1-2 hours)

Surface becomes tacky, no longer wet to touch

Foot Traffic (4-8 hours)

Safe for walking, light foot traffic only

Vehicle Traffic (24-48 hours)

Safe for normal vehicle use and parking

Full Cure (30 days)

Maximum durability and chemical resistance

Factors Affecting Drying Time:

  • Temperature: 70-85°F = optimal | Above 90°F = too fast | Below 50°F = too slow
  • Humidity: High humidity extends drying time significantly
  • Wind: Light breeze helps, strong wind can cause surface defects
  • Thickness: Thicker applications take longer to cure
  • Material type: Asphalt emulsion dries faster than coal tar

Process & Application

What preparation is needed before seal coating?

Surface Cleaning (24-48 hours before)

  • Power washing - Remove dirt, debris, loose material
  • Oil stain treatment - Apply degreasing agents
  • Vegetation removal - Clear weeds, grass from cracks
  • Complete drying - No standing water or moisture

Structural Repairs (1-2 weeks before)

  • Crack filling - Seal all cracks wider than ¼ inch
  • Pothole repair - Fill and compact all holes
  • Edge repair - Fix crumbling edges and corners
  • Drainage issues - Correct standing water problems

Access Preparation (Day of service)

  • Vehicle removal - Clear all cars, equipment
  • Property protection - Cover plants, fixtures
  • Access routes - Clear path for equipment
  • Weather check - Confirm no rain in 48-hour forecast

DIY Advantages

  • Cost savings - Materials only: $0.05-$0.15/sq ft
  • Timing control - Work on your schedule
  • Quality control - Direct oversight of work
  • Learning experience - Gain maintenance knowledge

DIY Disadvantages

  • Equipment costs - Squeegees, sprayers, brushes ($200-$500)

  • Weather dependency - Must work within narrow weather windows

  • No warranty - Issues are your responsibility
  • Material waste - Hard to estimate exact quantities

Our Recommendation:

Small driveways (under 500 sq ft): DIY can be cost-effective if you have time and physical ability

Large areas (over 1,000 sq ft): Professional application recommended for best results and efficiency

Commercial properties: Always use professional contractors for liability and warranty protection

Maintenance & Care

How do I maintain seal coating to maximize its life?

Monthly Maintenance

  • Visual inspection - Check for new cracks or damage
  • Clean debris - Remove leaves, dirt, standing water
  • Oil stain check - Address spills immediately
  • Drainage assessment - Ensure proper water flow

Seasonal Maintenance

  • Power washing - Deep clean twice yearly (spring/fall)
  • Crack sealing - Fill any new cracks immediately
  • Edge maintenance - Keep borders clean and intact
  • Tree maintenance - Trim overhanging branches

Annual Assessment

  • Professional inspection - Comprehensive condition review
  • Surface evaluation - Check coating adhesion and wear
  • Drainage review - Assess and improve water management
  • Resealing schedule - Plan next application timing

What should I avoid doing on newly seal coated surfaces?

First 24-48 Hours - CRITICAL

  • No vehicle traffic - Cars will leave permanent marks
  • No foot traffic - Shoes can create impressions
  • No sprinklers - Water will damage uncured coating
  • No furniture - Heavy objects will indent surface

First 30 Days - CAUTION

  • Avoid sharp turns - Use gradual steering movements
  • No parking on hot days - Tires can soften coating
  • Clean spills immediately - Chemicals are more damaging
  • No pressure washing - High pressure can damage coating

Long-term Precautions

  • No metal objects - Shovels, tools can scratch surface
  • Careful with chemicals - Gas, oil, de-icers cause damage
  • Watch tire pressure - Low tires create scuffing
  • Avoid abrasives - Sand, salt, harsh cleaners

How do I clean and maintain my seal coated driveway?

Regular Cleaning (Monthly)

  • Garden hose rinse - Remove surface dirt and debris
  • Mild soap solution - Dish soap + water for general cleaning
  • Soft brush scrubbing - Use stiff bristle broom, not wire brushes
  • Thorough rinse - Remove all soap residue

Deep Cleaning (Seasonal)

  • Pressure washing - Low pressure (1,500-2,000 PSI) maximum
  • Degreasing solution - For oil stains and heavy soiling
  • Allow full drying - 24-48 hours before vehicle use
  • Inspection after cleaning - Check for damage or wear

Stain Removal Guide

  • Oil/Gas stains: Dish soap + hot water, scrub immediately
  • Rust stains: Oxalic acid cleaner (follow manufacturer directions)
  • Tire marks: Degreaser or WD-40, then soap and water
  • Paint spills: Remove while wet, use paint thinner if needed

Common Problems

Why is my seal coating peeling or flaking off?

Common Causes of Peeling:

Poor Surface Preparation

Cause: Applied over dirty, oily, or wet surfaces

Solution: Remove loose coating, clean thoroughly, reapply

Prevention: Proper cleaning and drying before application

Temperature Issues

Cause: Applied in temperatures below 50°F or above 90°F

Solution: Wait for proper conditions, remove and reapply

Prevention: Follow temperature guidelines strictly

Too Many Coats

Cause: Multiple applications without proper curing time

Solution: Remove excess layers, allow proper curing

Prevention: Limit to 2-3 total coats maximum

What causes streaks or uneven appearance in seal coating?

Application Problems

  • Uneven thickness - Inconsistent spreading technique
  • Lap marks - Overlapping wet and dry edges
  • Tool marks - Improper squeegee or brush technique
  • Contamination - Dirt or debris mixed during application

Material Issues

  • Poor mixing - Inadequate agitation of sealer
  • Old material - Expired or improperly stored sealer
  • Wrong dilution - Improper water/additive ratios
  • Quality variations - Cheap or inconsistent products

Prevention Strategies:

  • Use professional-grade equipment and materials
  • Maintain wet edge during application
  • Work in manageable sections
  • Ensure proper material mixing and agitation
  • Apply in suitable weather conditions

Is it normal for seal coating to track or stick to shoes/tires?

Normal Tracking (First 30 days)

  • Light shoe marks - Minimal transfer on clean shoes
  • Hot weather softness - Slight tackiness on very hot days
  • Initial tire marks - Light scuffing during first week
  • Gradual hardening - Decreasing tackiness over time

Problem Tracking

  • Heavy transfer - Coating coming off on shoes/tires
  • Persistent stickiness - Remains tacky after 48+ hours
  • Color transfer - Black marks on light surfaces
  • Surface damage - Footprints or tire impressions remain

Solutions for Excessive Tracking:

  • Allow more cure time - Extend traffic restrictions
  • Apply sand additive - Reduces surface tackiness
  • Professional assessment - May indicate application problems
  • Avoid hot pavement - Don't walk on it during peak heat
