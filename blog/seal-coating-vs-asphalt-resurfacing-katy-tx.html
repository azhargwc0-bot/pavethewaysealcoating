What is Seal Coating vs Asphalt Resurfacing?

Seal Coating Definition

Seal coating is a protective liquid coating applied directly to existing asphalt surfaces. It acts like sunscreen for your driveway, protecting against UV rays, water damage, oil spills, and normal wear. The process involves cleaning, crack filling, and applying 1-2 thin coats of sealer.

Best for: Preventive maintenance on asphalt in good to fair condition (less than 10 years old with minimal cracking).

Asphalt Resurfacing Definition

Asphalt resurfacing (also called overlay) involves applying a new 1.5-3 inch layer of hot asphalt over the existing pavement. This process addresses structural issues and provides a completely renewed surface.

Best for: Restoring severely damaged asphalt with extensive cracking, drainage issues, or structural failure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I seal coat over recently resurfaced asphalt?

Yes, but wait 6-12 months for new asphalt to cure fully. Fresh asphalt contains oils that prevent proper sealer adhesion. After curing, seal coating extends the resurfacing investment.

How do I know if my driveway needs resurfacing vs seal coating?

If you can see the aggregate (stones) through the asphalt surface, have cracks wider than ¬º inch, or notice water pooling, resurfacing is likely needed. Minor surface cracks and fading indicate seal coating is sufficient.

What's the best time for each service in Katy TX?

Seal Coating: September-October and March-April when temperatures are 50-80¬∞F with low humidity. Resurfacing: October-March avoiding extreme heat and humidity.

Can seal coating fix drainage problems?

No. Seal coating follows existing surface contours. If your driveway has drainage issues with standing water, resurfacing with proper grading is necessary to correct the slope.
