üßÇ Ice Management Best Practices

Safe Deicing Options for Asphalt:

  • Sand: Provides traction without chemical damage (BEST OPTION)
  • Calcium Chloride: Less harmful than rock salt, works at lower temperatures
  • Magnesium Chloride: Gentler on asphalt than sodium chloride
  • Kitty Litter: Emergency traction option, clay-based varieties only

Materials to AVOID:

  • Rock Salt (Sodium Chloride): Can damage asphalt binders and aggregate
  • Metal Scrapers: Scratch and gouge asphalt surfaces
  • Boiling Water: Thermal shock can crack cold asphalt
  • Chemical Solvents: Can dissolve asphalt binders

‚ùì Winter Protection FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions
How does freezing weather damage asphalt in Texas?
Freezing weather damages Texas asphalt through freeze-thaw cycles where water in cracks expands 9% when frozen, creating pressure that widens cracks and causes structural damage. Thermal contraction also creates stress at weak points throughout the surface.
Should I use rock salt on my asphalt driveway in Texas?
Avoid rock salt on asphalt driveways in Texas. Use sand for traction or calcium chloride ice melter instead. Rock salt can damage asphalt surfaces and is often unnecessary given Texas' brief freezing periods.
What should I do if my driveway cracks during a Texas freeze?
Document new cracks with photos but wait for temperatures above 50¬∞F before repairs. Apply temporary crack sealer if temperatures allow, then schedule professional repair when weather permits proper material application.
How can I prevent ice formation on my Texas driveway?
Prevent ice formation by ensuring proper drainage, sealing cracks before winter, applying fresh seal coat, and using sand for traction. Eliminate standing water in low spots and maintain clear drainage paths.
Is winter seal coating possible in Texas?
Winter seal coating is not recommended when temperatures drop below 50¬∞F. Complete seal coating before winter arrives, typically by late November. Emergency repairs may be possible during brief warm spells.
How long does freeze damage take to appear?
Freeze damage can appear immediately during the freeze or become visible after thawing. Some damage develops over multiple freeze-thaw cycles. Inspect thoroughly after each freeze event and document changes.
